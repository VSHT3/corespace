# CAS (Creativity, Activity, Service) — IB Guide

## What is CAS?
CAS is one of the three core components of the IB Diploma Programme. Students must complete a minimum of 18 months of CAS activities and write reflections demonstrating engagement with all 7 learning outcomes.

## Three Strands
- **Creativity**: arts and other experiences involving creative thinking. Examples: painting, music, photography, coding, cooking, design.
- **Activity**: physical exertion contributing to a healthy lifestyle. Examples: sports, hiking, dance, yoga, gym.
- **Service**: collaborative and unpaid community engagement. Examples: tutoring, volunteering, charity work, environmental projects.

## 7 Learning Outcomes (LOs)
Students must demonstrate evidence for all 7:
1. Identify own strengths and areas for growth
2. Demonstrate undertaking new challenges
3. Demonstrate planning and initiating activities
4. Show commitment to and perseverance in CAS activities
5. Demonstrate skills and recognise benefits of working collaboratively
6. Demonstrate engagement with issues of global significance
7. Recognise ethical implications of choices and actions

## Reflections
- Written reflections are mandatory evidence for each LO
- Can be text, video, images, or audio (platform-dependent)
- Should be honest, specific, and analytical — not just descriptive
- Must reference specific LOs with explicit reasoning

## Hours & Duration
- No specific minimum hours — quality over quantity
- Must span 18 months
- Variety and genuine engagement matter to examiners

## Common Student Mistakes
- Listing activities without genuine reflection
- Not mapping activities to specific LOs
- Only choosing easy/comfortable activities (no challenge)
- Starting CAS documentation late (semester 4 panic)
- Ignoring Service strand

## IB Examiner Expectations
Examiners look for: personal growth, challenge, genuine commitment, LO mapping, and evidence of reflection depth — not just activity volume.
