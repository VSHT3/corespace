# Extended Essay (EE) — IB Guide

## What is the EE?
The Extended Essay is an independent, self-directed piece of research of ~4,000 words submitted as part of the IB Diploma. It culminates in a viva voce (reflective conversation) with the supervisor.

## Key Requirements
- Word limit: 4,000 words (strict upper limit, examiners stop reading at 4,000)
- Target: 3,500–4,000 words for body content
- Must have: title page, contents page, introduction, body, conclusion, references/bibliography
- Research question (RQ): must be focused, specific, and answerable within the word limit
- Subjects: almost all IB subjects available (World Studies EE spans two subjects)

## Assessment Criteria (A–E, max 34 points)
- **Criterion A — Focus & Method** (6 pts): clear RQ, appropriate methodology
- **Criterion B — Knowledge & Understanding** (6 pts): subject-specific knowledge depth
- **Criterion C — Critical Thinking** (12 pts): analysis, argument quality, evaluation
- **Criterion D — Presentation** (4 pts): structure, formatting, bibliography
- **Criterion E — Engagement** (6 pts): evidenced through RPPF (Reflections on Planning and Progress)

## RPPF (Reflections on Planning and Progress Form)
- 3 mandatory reflection sessions with supervisor
- Max 500 words total
- Shows genuine intellectual engagement and growth

## Strong vs Weak EEs
Strong:
- Narrow, specific RQ that can genuinely be investigated
- Clear line of argument throughout
- Subject-specific methodology used correctly
- Honest evaluation of limitations

Weak:
- Broad RQ ("What is the impact of climate change?")
- Descriptive not analytical
- Missing methodology rationale
- No counter-arguments addressed

## Common Mistakes
- Starting too late (final EE due ~semester 4–5)
- Choosing a topic with no accessible sources
- Exceeding word limit (conclusion often cut)
- Not revising RQ after initial research
- Ignoring RPPF until the last minute

## Writing Process
1. Choose subject and broad topic area
2. Narrow to specific RQ (most important step)
3. Preliminary research → refine RQ again
4. Outline: intro → body sections → conclusion
5. Draft body chapters → supervisor feedback
6. Revise → introduction and conclusion last
7. Final proofread, format, bibliography
